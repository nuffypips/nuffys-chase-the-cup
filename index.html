Welcome aboard. I'm not gonna sugarcoat it — the last guy left us with a cap nightmare and a goalie with a 0.884 save percentage. So we're starting from scratch.

The league office is giving us a special dispensation: you can pull talent from any era in NHL history. The catch is they've equalized everything through a cross-era WAR model. A 1980s goal scorer doesn't get credit for padding stats in a 10-goal-a-game era. Everything gets normalized to modern standards.

Specifically: scoring is deflated for the high-flying 80s and inflated for the dead-puck 90s-2000s. Goalies are graded against their era peer average, not a universal number. Your final WAR total projects directly to a regular season win total, then we see how far we go in the playoffs.
